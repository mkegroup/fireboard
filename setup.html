Fire Ground Board is free for any department. You need one free Google account and about 5 minutes to get fully set up. This guide walks through every step.

Total setup time: 5 minutes
What you need
📱
A smartphone
Any iPhone or Android. Crew just need a browser — Safari or Chrome.
📶
Cell service
One bar is enough. The board syncs over regular cell data.
🔑
Google account
To create your free Firebase database. Takes 2 minutes if you don't have one.
💰
Budget: $0

Step-by-step setup
1
Go to Firebase Console
Open console.firebase.google.com in your browser and sign in with a Google account your department controls — a shared Gmail works fine.
2
Create a new project
Tap Add Project. Give it a name — something like BarreysBayFire or RenfrewFD. Click through the prompts. When asked about Google Analytics, you can skip it.
Each department gets their own project. Your incidents are completely private and separate from any other department using Fire Ground Board.
3
Add a Realtime Database
Once your project is created, navigate to:
Build → Realtime Database → Create Database
Choose a region — us-central1 is fine. When asked about security rules, select Start in test mode and click Enable.
What is "test mode"? It means anyone with your database URL can read and write for 30 days. That's fine — your 4-digit captain PIN protects control of the board, and you only share the URL with your crew. See the troubleshooting section below for how to renew after 30 days.
4
Copy your database URL
After the database is created you'll see a URL near the top of the screen. It looks like this:
https://your-project-default-rtdb.firebaseio.com
Copy that URL. This is the only thing you need to remember. Save it in your phone's notes app.
5
Open Fire Ground Board and start your first incident
Go to fire.mkegroup.ca. On the setup screen:
Department Name — your department name (e.g. Barry's Bay Fire)

Firebase Database URL — paste the URL from Step 4

Captain PIN — any 4-digit PIN. Share this only with your lieutenant or backup IC. Anyone with this PIN gets full captain access.

Incident Name — what this call is (e.g. Structure Fire Hwy 60)

Address — location of the incident
Tap Start Incident as Captain. You're live.
6
Share with your crew
Tap the 📲 button in the top right. You'll see a QR code and a share link. Post either one to your department group chat. Crew tap the link in Safari or Chrome — no download, no account needed. They tap their name and they're on the board instantly.
That's it. For every future call, your Firebase URL and PIN are pre-filled. You just enter the incident name and address and tap Start.
Troubleshooting
The board stopped syncing after a few weeks ▶
Firebase test mode rules expire every 30 days. Fix it in 30 seconds: go to Firebase Console → your project → Realtime Database → Rules tab. Replace everything with:

{ "rules": { ".read": true, ".write": true } }

Click Publish. Done. Set a monthly reminder on your phone so you don't get caught mid-call.
The Start Incident button isn't doing anything ▶
Two most common causes: 1) The Firebase URL is wrong — make sure it ends in .firebaseio.com with no trailing slash. 2) Your Firebase rules have expired — see the fix above. If neither helps, try reloading the page and waiting 5 seconds before tapping Start.
Crew scanning the QR code get sent to the setup screen ▶
This usually means the page is still cached from a previous version. Tell crew to close the tab completely and reopen the link, or open it in a private/incognito browser window. That bypasses the cache and loads fresh.
No cell service on scene — will it work? ▶
The board requires at least one bar of cell signal to sync between devices. One bar is enough. In a complete dead zone, the captain's phone still works as a local tracker — statuses update on their screen — and everything syncs automatically when signal returns. For scenes with zero signal, a physical PAR board is still your fallback.
Is our incident data private? ▶
Yes. Each department has their own Firebase project under their own Google account. The database URL is only shared with your crew. No data is shared with MKE Group, other departments, or anyone else. Your call history lives in your Firebase project and you can delete it any time from the Firebase Console.
Can multiple people have captain access? ▶
Yes. Anyone who enters the captain PIN on the login screen gets full control. Share the PIN with your lieutenant, deputy chief, or anyone who may need IC access. There's no limit on how many captain-level users can be active at once.
